About this Room

Welcome to Webflow for Beginners — a hands-on, live, interactive room for designers, entrepreneurs, freelancers, and creators who want to build beautiful, fully responsive websites using Webflow — no code required. Every session is recorded, so you can rewatch at your own pace.

This is not a passive lecture. You build alongside the instructor in real time, ask questions live, and leave every session with something tangible: a working page, a published layout, or a new skill you can immediately apply to client work.

🎯 Who This Room Is For

  • Designers who know Figma but have never shipped a live site
  • Freelancers who want to add Webflow to their service offering
  • Entrepreneurs who want to build and maintain their own website
  • Marketers who need to launch landing pages fast without relying on developers
  • Students and career changers entering the no-code / web design space
  • Anyone who tried Webflow before and felt lost — this room fixes that

📚 Full Course Index

The room runs as a structured course across weekly live sessions. Below is the complete index of what we cover:

Chapter 1 — Getting Started with Webflow
  • What is Webflow and how it compares to WordPress, Squarespace & Framer
  • Creating your free account and choosing the right plan
  • Tour of the Webflow Designer: canvas, left panel, right panel, Navigator
  • Understanding the HTML structure: Divs, Sections, Containers
  • Your first element: adding text, images, buttons, and links
  • Saving, previewing, and sharing your project
Chapter 2 — Styling & the Style Panel
  • How Webflow's Style Panel works (CSS under the hood)
  • Typography: font families, size, weight, line height, letter spacing
  • Colors, gradients, and managing your site's color palette
  • Spacing: padding, margin, and when to use each
  • Borders, shadows, and background images
  • Reusing styles with CSS Classes and Combo Classes
  • Global styles vs. element-level overrides
Chapter 3 — Layouts with Flexbox & Grid
  • When to use Flexbox vs. Grid
  • Building a navigation bar with Flexbox
  • Creating a hero section: centered layout, image + text side-by-side
  • CSS Grid for feature grids, card layouts, and image galleries
  • Auto columns, gap, alignment, and spanning cells
  • Nesting Flex and Grid containers
  • Common layout patterns: sidebar, split-screen, masonry-style grid
Chapter 4 — Responsive Design & Breakpoints
  • Understanding breakpoints in Webflow: Desktop, Tablet, Mobile Landscape, Mobile Portrait
  • How styles cascade down (and how to override at each breakpoint)
  • Building a hamburger menu for mobile
  • Hiding and showing elements per device
  • Testing your site on real devices using Webflow's preview tools
  • Common responsive mistakes and how to avoid them
Chapter 5 — Components, Symbols & Reusable Elements
  • Creating and managing global Symbols (now Components)
  • Building a reusable Navbar and Footer component
  • Overriding component content without breaking global styles
  • Button variants and style guides inside Webflow
  • Using the Asset Manager: uploading and organizing images, fonts, and SVGs
Chapter 6 — Webflow CMS
  • What is a CMS and when to use it
  • Creating a Collection: fields, types, and structure
  • Building a Blog with CMS: collection list, template page, and detail view
  • Portfolio and case study pages with dynamic data
  • Filtering and sorting CMS collections
  • Reference fields: linking blog posts to authors or categories
  • Multi-image fields and rich text blocks
Chapter 7 — Interactions & Animations
  • Overview of Webflow Interactions 2.0
  • Element triggers: on click, on hover, on scroll into view
  • Page load animations: fade in, slide up, stagger children
  • Scroll-triggered reveals and parallax effects
  • Lottie animations and how to embed them
  • Navbar scroll behavior: transparent → solid on scroll
  • Performance tips: keeping animations smooth and accessible
Chapter 8 — Forms, Integrations & E-commerce Basics
  • Building contact forms and connecting to Webflow Form submissions
  • Integrating with Mailchimp, ConvertKit, and Zapier
  • Embedding third-party tools: Calendly, Tally, Typeform, Google Maps
  • Introduction to Webflow E-commerce: products, cart, and checkout
  • Custom code embeds: when and how to use them safely
Chapter 9 — SEO, Performance & Accessibility
  • On-page SEO: title tags, meta descriptions, Open Graph images
  • Heading hierarchy and semantic HTML in Webflow
  • Image optimization: alt text, lazy loading, WebP format
  • Site speed: Webflow's built-in CDN and what it means for performance
  • 301 redirects and URL structure best practices
  • Accessibility basics: focus states, ARIA labels, color contrast
Chapter 10 — Publishing, Hosting & Going Live
  • Webflow.io subdomain vs. custom domain — what's the difference?
  • Connecting a custom domain: DNS settings, A records, and CNAME
  • SSL, HTTPS, and what Webflow handles automatically
  • Staging vs. production: using Webflow's publish workflow
  • Exporting clean HTML/CSS if you want to self-host
  • Handoff to clients: Editor mode, content manager permissions

💬 Weekly Discussion Topics

Each session ends with an open discussion. Past and upcoming topics include:

  • Should you use Webflow or Framer for client projects in 2025?
  • How to price a Webflow project: fixed, hourly, or value-based?
  • When does a no-code site need custom code — and where's the line?
  • Webflow vs. WordPress: which is better for content-heavy sites?
  • How to handle client feedback and revision cycles professionally
  • Building Webflow sites for clients who want to edit content themselves
  • Portfolio review: what makes a Webflow designer stand out in 2025?
  • Webflow University vs. paid courses — what's worth your time?
  • Is Webflow good for SaaS marketing sites? Real examples & teardowns

✅ Prerequisites

  • No coding experience required — this course is built for complete beginners
  • Basic computer literacy (you can use a browser and navigate folders)
  • A free Webflow account (we'll set one up together in Session 1)
  • Figma experience is helpful but not required
  • A laptop or desktop — Webflow Designer does not run on mobile

🛠 What to Prepare Before Your First Session

  • Sign up at webflow.com (free Starter plan is fine to start)
  • Join the room on BrainsMingle and enable notifications
  • Watch the optional 10-minute pre-read linked in the Discussion tab
  • Think of a real project you'd like to build — a portfolio, a landing page, or a small business site. We'll use it as your hands-on project throughout the course.
💡 Note from Sarah: Every session is recorded and added to the Recordings tab within 24 hours. If you miss a live session, you won't fall behind — just catch up on the recording and bring your questions to the next one.

👩‍💻 About the Instructor

Sarah Mitchell is a web designer and educator with 8+ years of experience building sites for startups, agencies, and Fortune 500 brands. She has delivered Webflow training to over 4,000 students and has been a Webflow Expert since 2019. Her work has been featured in Webflow's official Showcase and she runs a popular YouTube channel covering no-code design workflows.

Sarah's teaching style is direct, practical, and project-driven. No filler, no theory-only slides — every concept is demonstrated live with real builds.
